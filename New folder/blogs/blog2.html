9 Anime That Were Adapted Into Live-Action

Posted on: 11 JAN 2024

Anime adapted into live-action have gained popularity for several reasons. First and foremost, the existing fanbase of the original anime often contributes significantly to the success of its live-action adaptation. Fans are eager to see their beloved characters and stories brought to life with real actors and practical effects. Below are one of them.

one piece

The Netflix live-action adaptation of "One Piece" is an ambitious project headed by Steven Maeda and Matt Owens. Both seasoned writers and producers, Maeda has previously worked on shows like "Lost" and "The X-Files," while Owens contributed to Marvel's "Luke Cage" and "Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D." This dynamic duo brings a wealth of experience in creating captivating and complex narratives, showcasing their ability to handle intricate storylines and character development. Their involvement in the "One Piece" adaptation has generated considerable anticipation among fans, as they aim to translate Eiichiro Oda's vast and beloved pirate epic into a live-action format.

In the realm of live-action adaptations, "One Piece" faces the challenge of capturing the grandeur and whimsy of Oda's expansive world while maintaining the essence of the original anime and manga. The choice of Maeda and Owens, known for their work on high-stakes, character-driven narratives, suggests a commitment to delivering a compelling and faithful adaptation. The Netflix project has sparked both excitement and skepticism within the "One Piece" community, with fans eagerly awaiting the realization of iconic characters, epic battles, and the adventurous spirit that defines the anime. As the creators navigate the complex seas of translating a beloved anime into live-action, the "One Piece" live-action series on Netflix remains a highly anticipated venture, with expectations running high for an authentic and captivating portrayal of this beloved pirate saga.

attack on titan

"Attack on Titan," originally a manga series created by Hajime Isayama, has gained immense popularity and has been adapted into various formats, including an anime series and live-action films. The anime, directed by Tetsurō Araki and produced by Wit Studio (later MAPPA), is renowned for its intense narrative, well-developed characters, and the existential threat posed by colossal humanoid creatures known as Titans. The success of the anime led to the creation of two live-action films, "Attack on Titan: Part 1" (2015) and "Attack on Titan: End of the World" (2015), directed by Shinji Higuchi.

The live-action adaptations aimed to bring the thrilling and dystopian world of "Attack on Titan" to a new audience, utilizing practical effects and CGI to portray the massive Titans and the 3D Maneuver Gear-wielding Survey Corps. While the live-action films faced mixed reviews, with criticisms directed at the deviation from the source material and CGI limitations, they contributed to the overall global recognition of the "Attack on Titan" franchise. The anime, however, remains the primary and most acclaimed adaptation, captivating audiences with its dark themes, complex characters, and the relentless battle for humanity's survival against the Titans.

Tokyo Ghoul

"Tokyo Ghoul," originally a dark fantasy manga created by Sui Ishida, has been adapted into multiple formats, including an anime series and a live-action film. The anime, directed by Shuhei Morita, follows Kaneki, a college student turned half-ghoul after a chance encounter with one of these flesh-eating creatures. The intense narrative explores themes of identity, morality, and the coexistence of ghouls and humans. The live-action adaptation, released in 2017 and directed by Kentarō Hagiwara, condenses the complex storyline into a feature film.

The "Tokyo Ghoul" live-action film aimed to bring the dark and gory world of ghouls to a new audience while condensing the intricate plot of the manga and anime. The film faced mixed reviews, with some praising its visual elements and attempts to capture the essence of the source material, while others criticized its pacing and deviations from the original story. Despite the challenges of adapting such a complex narrative, both the anime and live-action film have contributed to the widespread recognition of "Tokyo Ghoul," solidifying its place as a notable series in the realm of dark fantasy and horror.

Parasyte

"Parasyte," originally a science fiction horror manga written and illustrated by Hitoshi Iwaaki, has been adapted into various formats, including an anime series and two live-action films. The anime adaptation, titled "Parasyte -the maxim-," directed by Kenichi Shimizu, follows the story of Shinichi Izumi, a high school student whose right hand is replaced by a parasitic alien. The anime delves into themes of identity, survival, and the coexistence of humans and parasites.

The live-action adaptation consists of two films, "Parasyte: Part 1" (2014) and "Parasyte: Part 2" (2015), directed by Takashi Yamazaki. These films condense the narrative into a cinematic experience, exploring the horror and psychological aspects of the original manga. The live-action adaptation received positive reviews for its special effects and attempts to capture the essence of the source material. While some deviations from the original story exist, the live-action films contributed to the overall popularity of "Parasyte" and introduced the intense and thought-provoking narrative to a broader audience. The success of both the anime and live-action adaptations solidified "Parasyte" as a standout in the realm of body horror and science fiction.

Ajin: Demi-Human

"Ajin: Demi-Human," an intense and thought-provoking manga series written and illustrated by Gamon Sakurai, has been adapted into an anime series as well as a live-action film trilogy. The anime adaptation, titled "Ajin: Demi-Human," directed by Hiroyuki Seshita and produced by Polygon Pictures, follows the story of Kei Nagai, an immortal "Ajin" with unique powers, as he navigates a world that fears and hunts beings like him.

The live-action film trilogy, directed by Katsuyuki Motohiro and Kohei Suruga, condenses the intricate narrative into a cinematic experience. Released in 2017 and titled "Ajin: Demi-Human - Compel," "Ajin: Demi-Human - Collision," and "Ajin: Demi-Human - Truth," the films capture the tension and moral dilemmas present in the original manga. The live-action adaptation received praise for its visual effects and its attempt to bring the unique Ajin abilities to life on the big screen. While some deviations from the source material exist, the trilogy introduced the compelling and dark world of "Ajin: Demi-Human" to a wider audience, further establishing the series as a noteworthy entry in the realm of supernatural and psychological anime adaptations.

Assassination Classroom

"Assassination Classroom," a popular manga series written and illustrated by Yūsei Matsui, has been adapted into both an anime series and a live-action film. The anime adaptation, directed by Seiji Kishi, follows the students of Class 3-E as they attempt to assassinate their alien teacher, Koro-sensei, before he destroys the Earth. The anime combines humor, action, and heartwarming moments, making it a well-received and successful adaptation.

The live-action film, titled "Assassination Classroom: Graduation" (2016), directed by Eiichirō Hasumi, serves as a sequel to the first live-action adaptation. The film continues the story, depicting the students' ongoing efforts to assassinate Koro-sensei while exploring the teacher's complex relationship with his students. The live-action adaptation received positive reviews for its entertaining blend of comedy and action, effectively capturing the spirit of the original manga and anime. Overall, both the anime and live-action adaptation of "Assassination Classroom" have contributed to the series' popularity, showcasing its unique premise and endearing characters to a diverse audience.

Kakegurui

"Kakegurui," an enthralling manga series, seamlessly made the transition to both anime and live-action formats, captivating audiences with its intense narrative centered around the dark and competitive world of gambling. The anime adaptation, under the direction of Yuichiro Hayashi, brilliantly brought to life the psychological warfare and high-stakes gambling battles that define the original manga. With vivid animation and dynamic storytelling, the anime not only maintained the suspenseful atmosphere of the manga but elevated it to a new level, earning praise for its portrayal of complex characters and adrenaline-pumping games.

The live-action adaptation of "Kakegurui," released in 2018, successfully retained the essence of the series while adding a distinct flair to the intense gambling scenes. Led by Minami Hamabe's compelling performance as Yumeko Jabami, the live-action drama proved to be a dynamic translation of the source material to the screen. Both adaptations have played a pivotal role in expanding the popularity of "Kakegurui," attracting a diverse audience with its unique premise, strategic plotlines, and the thrilling exploration of the psychological aspects of high-stakes gambling.

Your Lie in April

"Your Lie in April," a poignant and visually stunning anime, originated as a manga written and illustrated by Naoshi Arakawa. The anime adaptation, directed by Kyohei Ishiguro, follows the emotional journey of Kōsei Arima, a piano prodigy who has lost his ability to hear his own music after a traumatic event. The arrival of the vibrant and free-spirited violinist Kaori Miyazono brings color back into his life and reignites his passion for music. Renowned for its beautiful animation, evocative soundtrack, and touching narrative, the anime captivated audiences with its exploration of love, loss, and the healing power of music.

While "Your Lie in April" has yet to receive a live-action adaptation, its impact in the anime world has been profound. The series has garnered widespread acclaim for its emotional depth and the portrayal of complex relationships. It stands as a testament to the ability of anime to convey deeply moving stories, and its popularity has solidified its place as a beloved title within the medium.

Death Note

"Death Note," a psychological thriller manga and anime series, has left an enduring legacy through its thought-provoking narrative and complex characters. Tsugumi Ohba and Takeshi Obata's original manga, which centers around the moral dilemmas faced by Light Yagami after obtaining a supernatural notebook capable of causing death, captivated readers with its intricate plot and philosophical questions about justice and power. The anime adaptation, directed by Tetsurō Araki, further amplified the series' impact, delivering a visually stunning and emotionally charged experience that elevated "Death Note" to iconic status within the anime community.

The live-action adaptations of "Death Note," including a film series and television drama, aimed to bring the intense cat-and-mouse game between Light Yagami and L to the big screen. While these adaptations received mixed reviews due to deviations from the source material and pacing issues, they contributed to the broader cultural influence of "Death Note." The franchise's exploration of morality, the consequences of absolute power, and the blurred lines between right and wrong continue to resonate with audiences, solidifying "Death Note" as a hallmark in manga and anime adaptations.
